# India Export Policy Research 2024
### Evaluating the Export Promotion Measures of India

A ground-level research study combining government policy analysis with real interviews from active export business entrepreneurs across India.

This project critically evaluates how effectively India's export promotion schemes are reaching businesses on the ground — what works, what doesn't, and what needs to change. The research was conducted through direct conversations with export managers across diverse industries including automotive, pharmaceuticals, agriculture, and manufacturing.

*Originally completed as a university-level research assignment in Global Business. University details omitted for privacy.*

---

### Government Schemes Covered

| Scheme | Full Name | Core Benefit |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| MEIS | Merchandise Exports from India Scheme | Duty credit scrips based on export turnover |
| EPCG | Export Promotion Capital Goods Scheme | Zero customs duty on capital goods import |
| EOUs | Export Oriented Units | Excise and import duty exemptions |
| SEZs | Special Economic Zones | Full customs duty exemption and tax holidays |
| Duty Drawback | Duty Drawback Scheme | Refund of duties paid on export inputs |

### Key Findings

All five exporters actively use government schemes, yet face common barriers — particularly complex application processes and bureaucratic delays. Duty drawback rates were found to directly influence how exporters price their products internationally. EOUs and SEZs were valued most for their tax exemptions and infrastructure support. Every respondent highlighted the need for simplified processes, faster disbursement, and better transparency from government bodies.

---

### Recommendations

The research recommends developing a user-friendly online portal for scheme applications, launching sector-specific awareness workshops, establishing dedicated helpdesks for exporters, implementing e-documentation to reduce paperwork, and ensuring greater alignment between government policy and actual industry needs.
